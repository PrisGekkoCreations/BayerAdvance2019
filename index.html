Rottnest Island

12.00pm – 6.00pm

Coach transfers will depart The Westin Perth at approx. 12noon for B-Shed, Victoria Quay Fremantle Ferry Terminal. Collect snorkel sets (optional) before boarding the Rottnest Express ferry to Rottnest Island (approx. 30mins). Upon arrival at Rottnest Island, disembark and collect hire bikes (optional) from the main jetty. Enjoy free time exploring the beauty of this picturesque island. Ride or walk to one of the 63 beaches and 23 bays located around the island or get in the water with your snorkel and explore Rottnest Island’s underwater playground. During registration you will select one of the following tour options.

Option #1 – Adventure Tour

60 min Adventure Boat Tour
1.45pm Group 1
3.00pm Group 2

Jump aboard the purpose-built, high-powered vessel for the adventure of a lifetime as the Eco Express takes you for a 60-minute journey to the island's wild side. Explore Rottnest's rugged coastline and witness the beauty of its abundant wildlife up close, including the delightful antics of the New Zealand fur seal colony at Cathedral Rocks. With our knowledgeable crew providing expert commentary along the way, this is the ultimate thrill ride for nature lovers!

Option #2 – Discover Tour

60 min Discover Coach Tour
1.45pm Group 3
3.00pm Group 4

This 60-minute coach tour takes you to some of Rottnest’s most spectacular locations, where you can experience the island's diverse flora, fauna, history and culture for yourself (with insightful commentary from knowledgeable crew along the way). The Discover Rottnest Coach Tour stops at key vantage points around the island, where passengers can hop off the coach and stretch their legs as they soak up some of Rottnest's world-famous views. Chances are, while you are there, you're going to meet a Quokka or two!

Option #3 – Explorer Tour

60 min Sub Sea Explorer Semi-Submersible Vessel
1.45pm Group 5
3.00pm Group 6

Take a journey of a lifetime on the iconic coral viewing vessel, the 'Sub Sea Explorer' and venture beneath the waves of Rottnest Island to discover the amazing story of shipwrecks and the incredibly rich marine life that now inhabit them. Watch from below the waterline to spot as many fish and marine species as you can. You will probably see many species of fish, rays and perhaps spot one or two of the new tropical species that now call Rottnest home.

Following the tours and free time, return your hire bikes and snorkel equipment to the main jetty before boarding the Rottnest Express ferry for our return to the mainland. Upon arrival at B-Shed Victoria Quay Fremantle Ferry Terminal, board coaches back to Westin Perth, arriving around 6.15pm.

Swan valley gourmet food & wine tour

1.00pm – 6.00pm

Coach transfers will depart Westin Perth at approximately 1.00pm and head towards the iconic Swan Valley. Throughout the afternoon, enjoy sampling some of the region’s best wines at Sandalford Winery, Lancaster and Mandoon Estate as well as gourmet produce including Nougat and Morish Nuts, Margaret River Chocolate and Lancaster Cheese. Detailed information on each stop is listed below. Approximate return to the hotel is 6.00pm.

1st stop Wine Tasting at Sandalford Wines

Sandalford Wines Swan Valley Estate has one of Western Australia largest cellar doors. As well as five stunning wine ranges, the Cellar Door is brimming with everything wine related from premium glassware, bottle openers and other ideas to local gifts.

2nd stop Nougat and Morish Nuts Tasting

Morish Nuts range have been created for over 20 years using a traditional family recipe prepared in the Swan Valley kitchen by artisans, using the finest Australian nuts. Offering a wide range of treats that are free of gluten, lactose, additives, preservatives and colourants.

3rd stop Margaret River Chocolate Factory

The Swan Valley factory and tasting centre offers free tastings as well as viewing windows to see all the action and hundreds of delicious chocolate products. Sister company Margaret River Providore is located on the same property, with a huge range of preserves, chutneys, olive oils, award-winning wines and a not-to-be-missed chocolate liqueur.

4th stop Lancaster Wine and Cheese Tasting

Located on the banks of the Swan River, Lancaster boasts some of the oldest vines in the Swan Valley. These vines produce Lancaster’s outstanding wines including Chenin blanc, Verdelho, Chardonnay, the legendary Old Vines Shiraz as well as the beautiful dessert wine – Late Picked Chenin.

Surround yourself in the vineyard experience, take your place at the bar of the famous outdoor tasting shed and enjoy tastings of Lancaster’s premium wines, together with a selection of local gourmet cheeses.

Kings Park Segway Tour

Coach transfers will depart Westin Perth at approximately 1.00pm and drop off at Barrack Street Jetty for safety briefing, segway training and helmet and headset fitting. Guides will ensure you are confidently ready to ride the segway before the tour commences.

Renowned as one of the World’s largest and most beautiful inner-city parks, Kings Park delights visitors with natural bush trails, open parklands, a stunning botanic garden and iconic views over the Perth city skyline. The tour departs from Barrack Street Jetty, meandering through the new Elizabeth Quay waterfront precinct before ascending Mt Eliza to reach the summit of Kings Park. Explore tree-lined avenues honouring Western Australia’s fallen soldiers and visit secluded parts of the park where native birds and animals are your travelling companions . The park’s botanic garden is one of the best locations for wildflowers in Spring and iconic views over the Swan River year round.

Return to Barrack Street Jetty where you will board transfer back to the hotel. Tour duration is approximately 90 minutes (plus transfers).

Note: maximum weight is 188kg and tour is not suitable for pregnant women.

Destination

Perth Western Australia

Situated between the lush wine growing country of the South West and the hypnotic Australian outback in the Kimberley region of the North, Perth is a breathtakingly beautiful city. Set on the banks of the spectacular Swan River and overlooked by Kings Park, Perth is one of Australia's fastest growing urban centres.

Laid-back, liveable Perth has wonderful weather, beautiful beaches and an easygoing character. Perth combines big-city attractions and relaxed, informal surrounds, providing an appealing lifestyle for locals and lots to do for visitors. It's a sophisticated, cosmopolitan city, with myriad bars, restaurants and cultural activities all vying for attention. Perth's pristine parkland, nearby bush, and river and ocean beaches – along with a good public-transport system – allow its inhabitants to relax and enjoy what's on offer.

On average, Perth receives 3000 hours of sunshine per year. Although January is the hottest month of the year, it is considered the perfect month to visit. Temperatures average 25 degrees and the sea water temperature hovers around 21 degrees. January temperatures may reach up to 40 degrees; nevertheless, humidity remains low.
